<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What blockchain is Reflect available on?" icon="question">
    <u>reflect.money</u> is available primarily through the **solana network** but can be accessed without a wallet via third-party applications built using our systems. 
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does reflect issue its own stablecoins?" icon="question">
    **No, reflect does not issue its own stablecoins** - instead reflect issues interest-generating versions of existing trusted assets or aggregated baskets of them.  
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Where does the interest come from?" icon="question">
    **Reflect primarily generates interest through onchain strategies** that maximise the capital efficiency of an asset. The interest-generation strategy for each asset is specific to providing the lowest risk returns for its available markets. 

    _more details can be found within the specific stablecoin(s) documents page._
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is it safe to deposit assets to Reflect?" icon="Question">
    Reflect uses verifiable, onchain programs to manage its deposited assets - **these programs have been and continue to be rigorously audited** by industry leading security firms helping to minimise risk.

    However, there are inherent risks to using onchain finance and self-custody that you should understand before using our application. 
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does reflect.money showcase APY?" icon="Question">
    **reflect.money showcases the realtime interest rate** - the yearly rate derived from the actual amount of interest generated on a second-by-second basis. _This is the most fair, transparent way to showcase yields without misleading users with things such as trailing averages. _
